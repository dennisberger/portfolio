# Design Context

## Users

Three audiences, in priority order:

1. **Design VPs and leaders** evaluating Dennis as a peer, collaborator, or hire. They arrive skeptical, read fast, and pattern-match on craft. They will decide whether to take the site seriously in under 10 seconds and then either dig in or bounce.
2. **Hiring managers and recruiters** scanning 30+ portfolios in a session. They need to find recent experience, seniority signals, and evidence quickly. They skim more than read.
3. **Design peers and curious visitors** (from LinkedIn, referrals, speaking context). They are the warmest audience — already predisposed to like him — and they're the ones who will share the site if it's memorable.

**Context of use**: laptop browser, daytime, work hours. Some mobile. Rarely printed.

**Job to be done**: Convince the reader that Dennis is an interesting, serious operator who would be fun to work with — in a space saturated by AI-templated portfolios that all feel the same.

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: considered, wry, unimpressed by hype.

**Fuller voice**: Someone who writes HTML better than Spanish, speaks fluent operational systems, rolls his eyes at corporate bullshit without becoming cynical, and lets the human parts (cats, Aperol, Hawaiian pizza, anxiety about being punctual) stay visible instead of sanding them off. Anti-cult, pro-craft.

**Emotional goal**: a design VP closes the tab thinking "this person is interesting and sounds fun to work with — I want to know more." Not "impressive." Not "slick." Interesting and fun.

**What the brand is not**: Cynical. Precious. Templated. Reverent. Brutalist. Overly minimal. Crammed. Serious-in-a-sad-way. Trying too hard.

## Aesthetic Direction

**Positioning**: Editorial-systematic with a personality offset. Magazine structure meets DesignOps discipline meets "did this person really just put hot pink next to that?"

**Theme**: Ship light mode first, canonical and opinionated. Dark mode later, equally opinionated (not just color-inverted). Not both-at-once-equally-weighted.

**Color**: Blow up the current navy/terracotta/teal. New direction:
- **Mental image (anchor)**: a new beach condo on the Mediterranean. Black-framed windows, white walls, pops of color to bring the outside in. Minimal, graphic, confident. The sun and sea do the heavy lifting; the architecture just holds them.
- **Paper** — bright near-white with a whisper of warmth so it doesn't read clinical. Not cream. Not newsprint.
- **Ink** — near-black with warm undertone (~oklch 18% 0.02 60). Never pure #000.
- **Fuchsia / hot pink** — dominant accent. The 80s swishy-shorts energy. CTAs, links, the wry moments. Must clear 7:1 on paper for text uses.
- **Electric cobalt blue** — secondary accent. Spanish-tile adjacent. Evidence, structure, the serious stuff.
- **The tension is the brand**: pink is voice, blue is substance. Neither dominates alone.
- Neutrals tinted toward the ink hue, not cool gray.
- AAA text contrast where type exceeds 18px; AA elsewhere.

**Typography**: Both current fonts (Fraunces, DM Sans) are on the reflex-reject list and must be replaced.
- **Display**: Zodiak (Fontshare) as the working choice. Editorial serif with real personality, Didone-adjacent, italic carries the wry delivery.
- **Display fallback**: Gambarino (Fontshare) if Zodiak reads too "designer portfolio" once on screen — slab-display-hybrid, Spanish-poster energy.
- **Body**: Switzer (Fontshare). Swiss-inspired workhorse with more warmth than Helvetica/Inter-adjacent defaults.
- Modular scale, 1.25+ ratio, real weight contrast (not just one weight across the site).

**References that capture the right feel**:
- Bloomberg Businessweek covers — typographically bold, unafraid of color, confident.
- It's Nice That editorial layouts.
- Pentagram case-study pages (Mastercard, MIT Media Lab).
- Spanish graphic designers: Oscar Mariné, Manuel Estrada — bold color, editorial grids, Mediterranean.
- Magazines with personality: Apartamento, The Gentlewoman.

**Anti-references**:
- Designer-portfolio-noir-serif (Fraunces + Playfair + navy backgrounds).
- Vercel-brutalist / Linear-clone / Stripe-gradient.
- Squarespace-template.
- Any site that relies on radial-glow blobs, gradient italic hero words, hero-metric pill chips, or identical 3-stat card footers. (The current site is guilty of all four.)
- Walls of text. Cramming. Sans-serif-everywhere. Outdated. Slop.

## Signature Mechanics

Two devices carry the personality and the integrity — and they are *not decorative*, they are *functional*:

1. **Wry margin annotations.** Alongside the serious, numerate case-study copy, a second voice in the margin: footnotes, asides, the-way-you'd-explain-this-to-a-friend versions. Editorial convention, personality delivery vehicle. Sparing, specific, and always adding something the main copy doesn't.
2. **Show-your-receipts citations.** Every quantitative claim (SUS scores, budgets, adoption rates) gets a subtle numbered or marked reference that expands inline — source, method, caveat. Reinforces "unimpressed by hype" through evidence. This is the anti-puffery move.

**Rejected**: semantic HTML-tag labels on sections (`<role>`, `<reflection>`). Too try-hard for the space.

## Design Principles

Every design decision on this site gets checked against these three rules:

1. **Substance speaks first. Personality speaks second. Decoration speaks never.** Every stat is cited. Every image is deliberate. Every aside earns its place. No glows, no gradient text, no blur blobs.
2. **Editorial structure, bold color, wry voice — the tension is the point.** The grid is tight. The palette is loud. The prose is dry. If any one of those three drifts toward safe, the whole thing collapses into the template it's trying to escape.
3. **Show the work and the human.** Operational craft (cited evidence, visible systems thinking, DesignOps vocabulary) on one side; cats, Aperol, anxiety, Hawaiian pizza on the other. Don't sand either off. The whole pitch is that you can have both.

## Accessibility Commitment

- WCAG **AA** across the board as the floor.
- WCAG **AAA** for text contrast (7:1 body, 4.5:1 large) as the stretch.
- Real focus states, real skip links, real reduced-motion honor.
- Color is never the only channel carrying meaning.

## Technical Constraints

- Current stack: Vite + React 19, single-file inline-styled component. **Not a constraint** — user has green-lit a full rebuild, including possible migration to Astro.
- Foundation rebuild (tokens, type scale, file split, likely MDX for case studies) precedes all visual work.
- Rebuild target: Lighthouse 95+ across all categories on the light theme by default.
